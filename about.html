WELCOME

Nail Chami Public School (Tholdhar) A Unit of Bhilangana Welfare Society. The Nail Chami Public school was started in 2015 to address the problem faced by the local community regarding large distance the village childrenâ€™s travers to attend school.

The school was started in 2015 by Mr. Anuj Lal Shah. After being involved with the organisation, Emmanuel Hospital Association since 2013, Mr. Shah was filled with a sense of responsibility towards his own village and wanted to do something to improve the situation of his community. Thus, following discussions with other villagers he initially started the school in a rented accommodation. A year later four rooms were constructed and acted as the foundation of his dream of bringing accessible education to his village folk.

Mission

The mission of school founder is to deliver accessible schooling to the surrounding villages of his home community and stop migrating from rural to urban area.

Current Situation

The school currently is able to accommodate 107 students from many surrounding villages who otherwise needed to commute to far flung areas instead. A staff of five teachers works with the students currently, however their salary is an issue the school faces constantly. Approximately 30% of our students are taught without any fee since many of them are from families who live in poverty, Handicap father child, widower child.
